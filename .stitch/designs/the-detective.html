The Architect of Silence

"Every model has a tell. A bias in a weight matrix, a specific firing pattern in a residual stream. You just have to know how to listen to the silence between the activations."

In the vast, sprawling architecture of a trillion-parameter transformer, most see a black box. I see a crime scene. To the forensic researcher, weights are not just floating-point numbers; they are digital fingerprints, left behind by a training process that leaves no stone unturned and no bias unrecorded. My approach is less about engineering and more about pathology—dissecting the neural pathways to find the exact moment a model decides to lie, or the precise circuit where a concept is born.

Borrowing the diagnostic rigor of a clinic and the deductive logic of Baker Street, I treat activations as digital breadcrumbs. When a model exhibits emergent behavior, I do not look for more data; I look for the Attention Head that orchestrated it. Mechanistic interpretability is the art of proving that nothing in a neural network is accidental.

My methodology relies on Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) as high-resolution microscopes, peeling back the polysemantic layers of a hidden state to reveal the monosemantic truths beneath. It is a pursuit of the 'Logit Lens'—a way to see what the network is thinking before it even knows it's thinking it.
